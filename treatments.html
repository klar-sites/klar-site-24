Understanding Shoulder Injuries

The shoulder is the most mobile joint in the human body, making it highly susceptible to injury and wear. Whether caused by acute trauma, repetitive overhead movements, or age-related degeneration, shoulder conditions can significantly impact your daily life. At Nordic Shoulder Clinic, we provide a full spectrum of treatments tailored to the specific mechanics of this complex joint.

Common Symptoms

  • Persistent pain, especially when reaching overhead or sleeping on the affected side.
  • Stiffness and loss of range of motion.
  • Weakness in the arm or shoulder.
  • Popping, clicking, or grinding sensations during movement.
  • Instability or feeling that the shoulder might "slip out of place."

Primary Causes

  • Sports injuries from tennis, swimming, baseball, or weightlifting.
  • Repetitive strain from occupational tasks or manual labor.
  • Acute trauma, such as falls or direct blows to the shoulder.
  • Degenerative conditions like osteoarthritis or tendon wear-and-tear.
  • Poor posture and muscular imbalances over time.

Diagnostic Methods

Accurate diagnosis is the foundation of effective treatment.

Physical Examination

A comprehensive assessment of your range of motion, strength, and stability to identify specific points of pain and mechanical dysfunction.

Ultrasound Imaging

Dynamic, real-time imaging that allows us to visualize tendons and ligaments in motion, excellent for diagnosing rotator cuff issues.

MRI & X-Ray

High-resolution magnetic resonance imaging for soft tissue detail, and X-rays to assess bone structure and rule out fractures or arthritis.

Treatment Options

We exhaust conservative methods before recommending surgical intervention.

Physiotherapy

Targeted exercise programs, manual therapy, and modalities like ultrasound or TENS to reduce inflammation and restore function.

Injections

Corticosteroid injections for rapid pain relief, or regenerative treatments like PRP (Platelet-Rich Plasma) to stimulate tissue healing.

Arthroscopic Surgery

Minimally invasive procedures to repair torn tendons, remove bone spurs, or stabilize the joint, resulting in faster recovery.

Rehabilitation & Recovery Expectations

A structured, phased approach to ensure safe and lasting recovery.

Phase 1: Protection

Focuses on pain management, reducing inflammation, and protecting the injured tissue. Often involves immobilization or rest.

Phase 2: Mobility

Gentle, passive range-of-motion exercises to prevent stiffness and begin restoring normal joint mechanics.

Phase 3: Strength

Progressive resistance training to rebuild the muscles surrounding the shoulder, providing stability and support.

Phase 4: Function

Sport-specific or task-specific training to prepare the shoulder for the demands of your daily life or athletic activities.

Related Treatments

Rotator Cuff Repair

Surgical reattachment of torn tendons to the head of the humerus, followed by a structured rehab protocol.

SLAP Tear Repair

Arthroscopic stabilization of the labrum (the ring of cartilage surrounding the shoulder socket) to restore stability.

Shoulder Impingement Therapy

Conservative management focusing on posture correction, scapular stabilization, and activity modification.
