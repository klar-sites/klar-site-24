Our Treatments

Every shoulder is different. We offer a comprehensive range of evidence-based assessments and treatments — from conservative rehabilitation to post-surgical recovery — all personalised to your unique needs.

Shoulder Conditions We Treat

Understanding your condition is the first step towards effective recovery. Below are the most common shoulder problems we see.

Rotator Cuff Injuries

The rotator cuff is a group of four muscles and tendons that stabilise the shoulder. Injury can range from tendinitis and impingement to partial or full-thickness tears. Symptoms include pain when lifting the arm, weakness, and night pain. Treatment options include targeted strengthening, manual therapy, and in some cases coordinated post-surgical rehab.

Common causes: overuse, repetitive overhead activity, ageing, trauma.

Frozen Shoulder (Adhesive Capsulitis)

Frozen shoulder causes progressive stiffness and pain that severely limits movement. It typically develops gradually and can persist for months. Our approach combines gentle mobilisation, stretching, and pain management to gradually restore range of motion.

Phases: Freezing (painful), Frozen (stiff), Thawing (resolution). Early intervention can shorten the duration.

Shoulder Impingement Syndrome

Impingement occurs when the rotator cuff tendons are compressed during arm elevation. It causes a sharp, catching pain and weakness. Treatment focuses on correcting movement patterns, strengthening the rotator cuff and scapular stabilisers, and reducing inflammation.

Labrum Tears (SLAP & Bankart)

The labrum is a ring of cartilage that deepens the shoulder socket. Tears can result from trauma or repetitive overhead motion. SLAP tears affect the top of the labrum; Bankart lesions often accompany dislocation. Rehabilitation may be conservative or post-surgical, focusing on stability and strength.

Other Common Shoulder Problems

Tendinitis & Bursitis

Inflammation of the biceps tendon or subacromial bursa. We use load management and anti-inflammatory strategies to resolve pain.

Shoulder Dislocation & Instability

Recurrent dislocations require retraining of dynamic stabilisers. We build strength and control to reduce the risk of future episodes.

Arthritis of the Shoulder

Osteoarthritis or inflammatory arthritis can cause chronic pain. Our management includes mobility work, strength maintenance, and pain relief strategies.

Fractures & Post-Trauma

Following a clavicle or humeral fracture, we guide safe rehabilitation to restore function once healing is underway.

Nerve-Related Shoulder Pain

Conditions like thoracic outlet syndrome or cervical radiculopathy can refer pain to the shoulder. We assess and treat the root cause.

How We Diagnose

Accurate diagnosis is the foundation of effective treatment. We combine clinical expertise with modern tools.

Clinical Examination

A thorough history and physical exam, including specialised shoulder tests, to identify the likely pathology.

Diagnostic Imaging

We can refer for X-ray, ultrasound, and MRI when needed, and we are skilled in interpreting results in context.

Movement Analysis

Functional assessment of how your shoulder moves during daily and sporting activities to uncover contributing factors.

Our Treatment Approach

Every plan is built around your goals, your lifestyle, and the latest evidence.

Manual Therapy

Hands-on techniques including joint mobilisation, soft tissue release, and myofascial work to reduce pain and improve mobility.

Exercise Prescription

Targeted, progressive exercises to strengthen the rotator cuff, scapular stabilisers, and entire kinetic chain.

Load Management

Education on activity modification and gradual return to load, ensuring tissues heal without re-injury.

Post-Surgical Rehab

Structured protocols following rotator cuff repair, labrum surgery, shoulder replacement, or fracture fixation.

Sport-Specific Rehab

Return-to-sport programmes for athletes that simulate the demands of their sport in a controlled environment.

Pain Neuroscience Education

Understanding chronic pain through modern neuroscience to reduce fear and build confidence in movement.

What Recovery Looks Like

Healing times vary, but you'll always know what to expect. Here's a general guide for common shoulder conditions.

Condition Typical Sessions Expected Timeline Key Focus
Rotator cuff tendinitis 6–10 8–12 weeks Strengthening & load management
Frozen shoulder 8–14 3–12 months Mobility & pain modulation
Shoulder impingement 6–8 6–10 weeks Movement correction & posture
Post-operative (rotator cuff) 12–16 4–6 months Gradual tissue loading
Shoulder instability 8–12 3–6 months Dynamic stability & control

These are estimates. Your specialist will provide a personalised timeline after your assessment.

Frequently Asked Questions

Answers to common questions about our treatments and what to expect from your sessions.

Most shoulder conditions respond well to conservative (non-surgical) management. Surgery is considered only when high-quality rehabilitation has not achieved the desired outcome, or when a structural problem clearly requires it. We will always exhaust conservative options first and discuss all possibilities with you.
Early intervention is often beneficial. For acute injuries, we recommend starting within the first few days once any acute inflammation or fracture has been ruled out. Even if surgery is planned, prehabilitation can improve post-operative outcomes.
We continuously reassess your progress. If you plateau, we explore different strategies — this might mean adjusting your exercises, referring for repeat imaging, collaborating with your surgeon, or considering other modalities. You will never be left without a plan.
In most cases, modified activity is possible and even encouraged. We will guide you on what to avoid, what to modify, and when it's safe to return fully. Complete rest is rarely the answer for shoulder problems.
