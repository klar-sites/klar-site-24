Understanding Shoulder Injuries

An Overview of Shoulder Conditions

The shoulder is the most mobile joint in the human body, making it highly susceptible to injury and wear. Whether caused by acute trauma, repetitive strain, or age-related degeneration, shoulder pain can significantly impact your daily life.

At Apex, we specialize in diagnosing the exact source of your pain and implementing targeted treatments to heal the joint, not just mask the symptoms.

Common Causes & Risk Factors

  • Sports & Acute Trauma: Dislocations, fractures, and sudden falls.
  • Repetitive Strain: Overhead movements in sports or occupations.
  • Degenerative Conditions: Osteoarthritis and tendon wear over time.
  • Poor Posture: Chronic misalignment leading to impingement.
Assessment

Symptoms & Diagnostic Methods

Accurate diagnosis is the first step toward effective recovery. We use state-of-the-art technology to pinpoint your condition.

Recognizing the Symptoms

If you experience any of the following, it's time to consult a specialist:

  • Persistent pain that disrupts sleep, especially when lying on the affected arm.
  • Loss of range of motion or stiffness in the joint.
  • Weakness in the arm or inability to lift objects overhead.
  • Clicking, popping, or grinding sensations during movement.

Our Diagnostic Process

We combine physical assessments with advanced imaging:

  • Physical Examination: Assessing range of motion, strength, and pain points.
  • Ultrasound: Real-time imaging of tendons and soft tissues.
  • MRI: Detailed cross-sectional imaging for complex soft tissue tears.
  • X-Ray: Evaluating bone structure, fractures, and arthritis.
Treatment Modalities

Comprehensive Treatment Options

We offer a full spectrum of care, from conservative physiotherapy to advanced surgical interventions.

Targeted Physiotherapy

Customized exercise programs, manual therapy, and modalities like ultrasound to strengthen muscles and restore mobility.

Pain Management

Corticosteroid injections and nerve blocks to reduce acute inflammation and allow participation in physical therapy.

PRP Therapy

Platelet-Rich Plasma injections utilizing your body's own healing factors to accelerate tendon and tissue repair.

Arthroscopic Surgery

Minimally invasive procedures to repair rotator cuff tears, labral tears, and remove bone spurs with faster recovery times.

Joint Replacement

Total or partial shoulder replacement for severe arthritis or complex fractures to eliminate pain and restore function.

Post-Op Rehabilitation

Structured recovery protocols following surgery to ensure proper healing, prevent stiffness, and rebuild strength.

The Road to Recovery

Our Rehabilitation Process

Rehabilitation is a phased journey. We guide you through each stage, ensuring your shoulder heals correctly and regains full capacity.

Phase 1: Acute Management

Focus on reducing pain and inflammation while protecting the injured tissue.

Phase 2: Mobility Restoration

Gentle, progressive exercises to restore full range of motion and flexibility.

Phase 3: Strength Building

Targeted strengthening of the rotator cuff and surrounding stabilizer muscles.

Phase 4: Functional Return

Sport-specific or task-specific training to ensure safe return to activity.

Recovery Expectations

Recovery timelines vary based on the specific injury and treatment:

Tendinitis / Impingement 4-6 Weeks
Frozen Shoulder 3-6 Months
Surgical Recovery 4-6 Months

*Note: These are average estimates. Your personal timeline will be discussed during your consultation.
